Doğan Arslanoğlu

Art and media educator

I teach art, media studies, and design, and I build software for the classroom. My work concerns the conditions of attention in algorithmic culture: what it means to teach sustained looking, close reading, and deliberate making to students whose perceptual habits are formed by the feed. I take the feed seriously as a cultural form rather than treating it only as a distraction.

My current action-research project, Teaching After the Feed, examines what this means for classroom practice. The tools I build, collected at classroomtools.io, come out of that inquiry and are made for use in ordinary lessons.

Research

  • Teaching After the Feed. An action-research project on teaching in algorithmic culture.
  • Course Design in an Age of Algorithmic Attention. A workshop on designing courses that hold attention, with live demonstrations drawn from media studies and chemistry teaching.

classroomtools.io

  • classroomtools.io. A public collection of the classroom tools built from Teaching After the Feed.
    • Close Reader. A live annotation tool. Students tag and annotate any shared text in real time, and the class’s readings accumulate on screen as they work.
    • Constellation Board. A Warburg-inspired board for arranging images in relation to one another and finding patterns across them.
    • Attention Heatmap. Visualises where a class looks first on an image, poster, or artwork, aggregating responses live as students submit them.
    • JPEG Degradation. Makes lossy compression legible by running the degradation sequence in view of the class.

Apps & platforms

  • Cadence LMS. A modern, teacher-facing learning management system for any subject or course.
  • curatewith.art. A collaborative art-browsing tool built on open museum collections. Classes search across five institutions at once and build shared boards together in real time.
  • ExamLock. A secure in-class examination web app with a teacher dashboard and a student lockdown interface.

Teaching materials
